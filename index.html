NOAA Storm Prediction Center

SPC Convective Outlook Changes

Conditional Intensity Groups (CIG)
Effective March 3, 2026

TL;DR

  • SPC outlooks now answer two questions: "How likely?" AND "How intense?"
  • New hatch patterns on tornado, wind, and hail graphics show expected severity
  • Risk categories (Marginal → High) are unchanged
  • Three CIG levels for tornado & wind; two for hail
First product: 1630Z Day 1 Outlook, March 3, 2026

What's New — Conditional Intensity Groups

CIG levels use diagonal hatch patterns (not colors) overlaid on the existing probability areas. Higher CIG = denser hatching = more intense severe weather expected if storms occur.

Wind 3 CIG Levels

Three intensity groups for damaging winds. New 75% and 90% probability thresholds added for wind outlooks.

Higher CIG levels indicate greater likelihood of significant (≥75 mph) damaging winds if storms occur.

Hail 2 CIG Levels

Two intensity groups for hail (CIG 1 and CIG 2 only). CIG 3 is not used for hail outlooks.

Higher CIG levels indicate greater likelihood of significant (≥2 inch) hail if storms occur.

The Decision Matrix — Probability × CIG → Risk

This is the core operational change. The combination of probability and CIG level now determines the categorical risk level. Same probability can yield different risk categories depending on expected intensity.

What Stays the Same

The six risk categories and their colors are unchanged. CIG adds an intensity dimension — it does not replace the existing system.

TSTM MRGL SLGT ENH MDT HIGH
Category Color Description
TSTM Light GreenGeneral thunderstorm risk
MRGL Dark GreenMarginal risk of severe
SLGT YellowSlight risk — organized severe possible
ENH OrangeEnhanced risk — numerous severe
MDT RedModerate risk — widespread severe
HIGH MagentaHigh risk — severe outbreak expected

How to Read the New Outlooks

📊
"What are the chances?"
SPC probability areas → Risk category (colors)
⚠️
"How bad could it be?"
Conditional Intensity → CIG level (hatching)
Together: Complete severe weather picture — likelihood + intensity

Other Visual Updates

  • Cleaner categorical outlook map design
  • Text labels moved off map into separate legend
  • Same impact communication, improved readability
  • Hatch patterns are the only new visual element on the maps themselves
